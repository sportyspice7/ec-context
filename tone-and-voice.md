# Tone & Voice Guide — ExploreClairemont

## The Core Feeling
Everything written for ExploreClairemont should feel like it came from **a friendly, knowledgeable neighbor** — someone who grew up here, loves this community deeply, and wants everyone to feel like they belong.

## Primary Voice Characteristics

### Warm
- Welcoming language
- Feels like a hug, not a press release
- Uses "neighbor," "community," "we," "us" — inclusive framing

### Neighborly
- Conversational, not formal
- Like texting a friend who happens to know everything about Clairemont
- First names, casual phrasing, contractions are fine

### Community-First
- Celebrates locals, not brands
- Highlights people and places that make Clairemont special
- Roots for local businesses like a fan, not a reporter

### Authentic & Local
- Deep Clairemont knowledge — specific neighborhoods, local landmarks, real history
- Never generic "San Diego" framing when Clairemont-specific works
- Written by someone who actually lives here and cares

## What to Avoid
- Corporate or marketing speak
- Overly formal or journalistic tone
- Generic language that could apply to any city or neighborhood
- Hype without heart
- Anything that sounds like a press release

## Practical Examples

**Too formal:**
> "The Clairemont community is invited to attend an upcoming event at a local establishment."

**Just right:**
> "Hey neighbors — there's something fun happening this weekend right in your backyard!"

---

**Too generic:**
> "San Diego has a lot of great restaurants to explore."

**Just right:**
> "If you haven't been to [local spot] on Balboa yet, you're missing out — it's such a Bay Ho gem."

---

**Too corporate:**
> "Explorer Exclusives provides value-added offerings to community stakeholders."

**Just right:**
> "Explorer Exclusives is our way of saying thanks to neighbors — real deals from the local spots you already love."

## Newsletter-Specific (Fast Times)
- Always opens: **"Hey Neighbor!"**
- Reads like a weekly letter from a friend, not a digest
- Short paragraphs, easy to scan
- Celebrates what's happening, not just reports it

## Social Media (@exploreclairemont)
- Instagram/Facebook/TikTok
- Visual and community-driven
- Encourages sharing, tagging, participation
- Celebrates local moments, milestones, businesses
- Casual and fun — this is a neighborhood account, not a news outlet

## When in Doubt
Ask: *Would a friendly Clairemont neighbor say this?* If yes, you're on track.
